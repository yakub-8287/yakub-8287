2. Problem Statement: Common Challenges Facing Public Art Installations in Surat

Surat's climate, characterized by high humidity and temperature fluctuations, presents significant challenges to the structural integrity and aesthetic appeal of public art installations. Many common furniture pieces used in these spaces suffer from:

Wood rot and decay: Excessive moisture leads to damage in wooden structures, especially those exposed to the elements. Metal corrosion: Rust and oxidation affect metal components, compromising their strength and appearance. Fabric deterioration: Sun exposure and humidity cause fading and weakening of fabrics used in outdoor installations. Vandalism and accidental damage: Public spaces are vulnerable to damage from both intentional and accidental causes, requiring prompt and skillful repairs.

These issues not only detract from the artistic value of the installation but also pose safety hazards and lead to costly replacement or refurbishment projects.
