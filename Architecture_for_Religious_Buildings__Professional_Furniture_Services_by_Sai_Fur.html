Common Furniture Issues in Surat's Religious Buildings

Surat's humid climate and fluctuating temperatures pose significant challenges for the furniture in religious buildings. Wood, a common material in traditional structures, is particularly vulnerable to wood rot, termite infestation, and structural weakening. This can lead to costly repairs, safety hazards, and the loss of irreplaceable artifacts. Moreover, the heavy use of certain areas within temples and mosques means wear and tear can quickly exceed the lifespan of standard furniture. Ignoring these issues can lead to expensive emergency repairs and potential damage to historical or culturally significant items.
