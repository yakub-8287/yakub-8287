2. Problem Statement: The Challenges Facing Historical Furniture and Architecture in Surat

Surat's humid climate and fluctuating temperatures pose significant challenges to the preservation of historical furniture and architecture. Common issues include:

Wood rot and decay: High humidity leads to the growth of fungi and insects, weakening structural integrity. Cracks and splits in woodwork: Temperature fluctuations cause expansion and contraction, damaging delicate joints. Fading and deterioration of finishes: Sunlight and exposure to the elements cause paint and varnish to peel and crack. Insect infestation: Termites and other insects damage the wooden components, making them unstable.

Ignoring these problems can lead to costly repairs, safety hazards, and the irreversible loss of valuable artifacts. Protecting your historical investments is crucial for preserving Surat's unique cultural identity.
